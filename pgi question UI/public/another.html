The nurse is performing open endotracheal suctioning for a client with a tracheostomy tube. Which of the following actions by the nurse are appropriate? Select all that apply.

Explanation

Administers 100% oxygen prior to suctioning the client: Preoxygenate with 100% oxygen to reduce the risk of hypoxemia.

Applies suction while withdrawing the catheter from the airway: Apply suction only while withdrawing to minimize trauma and prevent airway collapse.

Instills sterile normal saline into the tracheostomy prior to suctioning: No longer supported by evidence, increases the risk of infection.

Limits suctioning to 20 seconds during each suction pass: Should be limited to 10 seconds or less to reduce the risk of hypoxemia and trauma.

Uses sterile gloves and technique throughout the procedure: Essential to prevent infection.
